The Boy Who Cried Wolf

A shepherd boy repeatedly tricks villagers into thinking a wolf is attacking his flock. When a wolf actually appears, no one believes him. The moral: Liars are not believed even when they tell the truth.

The Tortoise and the Hare

A speedy hare mocks a slow-moving tortoise. They race, and the hare, overconfident, naps during the race. The tortoise wins by steady and consistent progress. The moral: Slow and steady wins the race.
